# Safety and data handling

- Collect only public business data. User approval grants browser access but does not turn an ambiguous personal page into a business source.
- Keep source URLs and field-level evidence with every record.
- Never collect private messages, session tokens, cookies, financial data, health data, or protected-class attributes.
- Reject URLs containing authentication, session, signed-link, or token query parameters; do not include them in tab lists, evidence, or exports.
- Do not infer sensitive traits or use them for lead scoring.
- Respect source terms, robots directives where applicable, rate limits, privacy law, consent requirements, suppression lists, and deletion requests.
- Use low collection limits and user-visible browser activity. Stop when challenged or blocked.
- On LinkedIn, use only public company and school pages. Never collect personal profiles, feeds, messages, connections, account UI, or inferred personal traits.
- On other websites and social pages, require explicit organization or business-page evidence. Stop when the page is ambiguous.
- Keep extraction separate from outreach. This skill does not send messages, follow accounts, post content, or automate engagement.
- Review exports before transferring them to a CRM or third party.
- Delete local records when they are no longer needed.
